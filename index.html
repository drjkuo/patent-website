Why LLMs here

Speed without pretending the machine is counsel

We use LLM-accelerated workflows to produce consistent, review-ready drafts. We do not output legal doctrine (for example, no motivation-to-combine narratives, KSR-style storytelling, or analogous-art arguments). Uncertain technical matches are flagged for human review—because a wrong “yes” costs more than an honest “review this.”

Embeddings (plain English)

Embeddings turn patent text into a mathematical “meaning map.” That helps surface the paragraphs in a large prior-art set that are most likely to matter to a specific limitation—faster first pass, fewer missed corners—without claiming perfect recall.

Frontier models (plain English)

Today’s frontier LLMs are strong at structured outputs, long-document comparison, and stable tabular formatting. We use that to accelerate matrices and chart drafts your team can take into a conference call—not to replace claim construction or courtroom judgment.

What you should expect

  • Draft-style disclosure matrices, claim-chart style writeups, and gap tables aimed at technical mapping.
  • Independent-claim focus aligned with how your invalidity prep often starts.
  • Explicit boundaries: attorney review, legal strategy, and advocacy remain yours.

Service scope

  1. Intake: patent number, goals, and any constraints you want honored.
  2. Technical mapping pass: candidate references structured against independent limitations.
  3. Review package: drafts you can mark up, challenge, and fold into your legal theory.

Timelines and deliverable formats are confirmed after intake—this page is not a binding services contract.
